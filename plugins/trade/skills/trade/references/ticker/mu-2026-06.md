# MU FY26Q3 Earnings Trade Case Study (June 2026)

A blowout memory-supercycle print where the analyst (me) was right pre-earnings, then got direction wrong **after** the print by reading it off an early after-hours snapshot, then flipped back to bullish when the user pushed back and the data updated. Net structural lesson: **post-print, the data has a time axis — an early AH snapshot expires within minutes, and full-day flow is contaminated by pre-event positioning. Pull the *post-event* tape + flow before calling direction.** Direct sister case to `nok-2026-04.md` (Error 1): pattern-recognition published ahead of the data that would falsify it.

---

## Setup

- **Ticker**: MU
- **Event**: FY26Q3 (May Q) earnings, **2026-06-24 AMC**
- **Starting context (pre-earnings, 6/18)**:
  - Stock **$1,134 (+8.85% on the day)** — parabolic: $864 (6/5) → $1,134 (6/18), ≈9× in a year off the ~$123 (Jun 2025) base
  - **IV Rank ≈ 100** (1-yr max), IV 122% on the 6/26 (front earnings) expiry; term structure front-loaded (6/26 122% > 7/2 113% > 7/10 106%) = **event-driven IV**
  - **Implied move ±12.35% (±$140)** → ~$994 ↔ $1,274 for 6/26
  - **Net options premium 6/18: +$341M net directional** (net call +$335M, net put −$6M / puts sold) — extreme bullish flow, dwarfing the +$15M NOK signal
  - Earnings beat history **5-for-5**: Q2 $12.20 vs $9.19 (+33%), Q1 $4.78 vs $3.96 (+21%), then +6%, +19%, +9%
  - Consensus EPS $19.72 / rev $35.0bn; Funda house preview aggressive at EPS $21.6 / rev $37bn
  - Bull-conviction count ≈ 5 (sector re-rate, beat history, new-info-likely, extreme bullish flow, implied<realized) — BUT **"de-risked" was a hard NO** (parabolic, not beaten-down); priced-in very high

## Strategy Evolution

### Stage 1: Pre-earnings structure (6/18, $1,134) — **analysis correct**

**Thesis**: bullish but heavily priced-in; the single highest-confidence fact is IVR 100 event-IV that *will* crush post-print.
**Structure**: core **bull put spread 6/26 $1,000/$950** (credit ~$10.6, short vega, asymmetry-rule-approved bullish structure) + satellite **call debit spread 6/26 $1,200/$1,300** (defined-cost tail-catcher for the Funda blowout). Ran the bull-conviction count + counterfactual P/L matrix; explicitly banned Jade Lizard / IC (conviction ≥ 4) and naked long calls (vega tax at IVR 100).
**In hindsight**: correct. The print + AH reaction validated this exact bullish setup. Three axes aligned, defined risk both ends.

### Stage 2: Post-print direction call off an early AH snapshot (6/24) — **the analyst error**

**The print** (blowout, beat even Funda's aggressive numbers):
- Rev **$41.456B** vs consensus $35.8B = **beat 16%** (and > Funda's $37bn)
- EPS **$25.11** vs $20.71 = **beat 21%**; +1,215% YoY, +106% QoQ

**The tape I was looking at**:
- Regular close $1,046.75 (−0.48%), intraday low $991 (briefly under $1,000)
- AH snapshot at **16:05 ET: $1,084.91 (+3.6%)**
- Full-day net flow: **−$3.4M** (net seller)

**My (wrong) read**: "A 16% revenue beat that tops even Funda's aggressive view, and the stock only manages +3.6% AH — that's priced-in exhaustion / sell-the-news." Revised probabilities to bear 35% / bull 25%, **withdrew the bullish stance, made bear call spread the primary, and declared the legacy call debit spread "dead."**

**Why it was wrong** (the data was misleading me, and I let it):
1. **The +3.6% snapshot was already stale.** AH was *accelerating*, not settling. Seven minutes later it was +9%.
2. **Full-day flow (−$3.4M) was contaminated by pre-event positioning** — daytime profit-taking/de-risking into a parabolic print. The post-event window told the opposite story.
3. **I conflated "muted *relative to the beat*" with "bearish *in absolute direction*."** AH was green the whole time; "the beat didn't push it to a new high" is a priced-in *magnitude* read, not a direction-reversal signal.

### Stage 3: User pushback + flip (6/24) — **correction**

**User**: "但是盘后依然在涨，你确定是空吗" (it's still rising after hours — you sure it's short?)

**Re-pulled data (should have done before publishing Stage 2)**:
- AH **16:13 ET: $1,141.36 (+9.04%)** — accelerated from +3.6%; now within −5.8% of the 6/22 high $1,211
- **Post-event flow (last 30 min into the close + AH): +$72.5M net bullish** (net call +$43.6M, net put −$28.9M / puts sold). The −$3.4M was a full-day artifact; money rushed back in the moment the beat printed.

**Correction**: flipped direction back to bullish (pitfall 20 continuation default), **withdrew the bear call spread** (selling calls at +9% into a 5%-from-high tape with +$72.5M inflow = fighting the tape), revived the call debit spread, kept the bull put spread (now safer). Probabilities back to bull ~45% / base ~35% / bear ~20%. Held the line on caveats: thin AH liquidity, T+1 must hold $1,100, valuation cycle-top is a *medium-term* risk, don't chase the +9% AH print.

## What Worked

1. **Pre-earnings (Stage 1) analysis was correct** — three-axis-aligned bull put spread + call-spread tail, defined risk, asymmetry-approved. The print validated it.
2. **Took the pushback and re-ran the data immediately** (same as NOK) — flipped within one turn, said "you were right, I was wrong" plainly.
3. **Held caveats after flipping** — did not over-correct into chasing the +9% AH print; kept the T+1-confirmation and cycle-top-valuation guards.

## What I Got Wrong (Analyst Side)

1. **Read direction off a single early AH snapshot.** AH prices in the first 30 min are noise (my own `strategies.md` says so) and *evolve* — +3.6% at 16:05 became +9% at 16:13. Should have sampled the trend, not one point.
2. **Used full-day flow instead of post-event flow.** −$3.4M for the day was dominated by pre-print de-risking; the post-event window was +$72.5M bullish. After a catalyst lands, the pre/at-event flow is a different regime — read the flow *since* the print.
3. **Confused "muted relative to the beat" with "bearish in absolute terms."** The stock was up the whole time. "Priced-in magnitude" ≠ "direction reversal."
4. **Same meta-root as NOK Error 1**: published a pattern ("blowout beat + muted reaction = sell-the-news") ahead of the current data that would falsify it.

### Meta-insight

NOK taught "pull flow + catalyst-clock before calling a fade." MU adds the **time-axis refinement**: post-print, *which* data you pull matters as much as *whether* you pull it.
- **Snapshot vs trend**: an AH quote is a moving target for the first 30–60 min; sample it more than once before committing to direction.
- **Full-day vs post-event flow**: a catalyst splits the session into two regimes; pre/at-event flow (de-risking, hedging) contaminates the full-day number. Use the post-event slice.
- **Absolute direction vs reaction-magnitude**: "smaller than the beat deserved" is a priced-in read; it does not flip the sign of the move.

## Reusable Framework: Reading Direction Off a Post-Print Tape

1. **Sample the AH price trend, not a point.** Pull the quote ≥2× over several minutes. Rising-and-accelerating ≠ "muted." Note the level vs the pre-earnings high (reclaiming it = continuation; stalling well below = priced-in).
2. **Pull post-event flow, not full-day.** Slice the tape from the print forward (or the last 30 min into the close + AH). A full-day net that's flat/negative can hide a strongly bullish post-event slice (here −$3.4M day vs +$72.5M post-event).
3. **Separate magnitude from direction.** "Beat was huge but reaction is only +X%" is a *priced-in* statement (how much was already in), not a *direction* statement (which way next).
4. **Confirm at T+1 regular session.** AH liquidity is thin; require the open to hold the key level (here $1,100) and the post-print high to be defended before sizing up. Guard for pitfall 10 (T+1 reverse drift) on AH spikes.
5. **Keep medium-term risks as context, not the trade.** Cycle-top valuation (here consensus FY27→FY28 EPS flat = a plateau) is a real medium-term worry but does not override the short-term tape (Core Principle 1: tape > opinion > DCF).
