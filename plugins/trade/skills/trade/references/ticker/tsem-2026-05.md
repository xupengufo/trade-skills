# TSEM Q1 2026 Earnings Trade Case Study (May 2026)

Pre-earnings setup had high-conviction directional bull view from proprietary research (well above sell-side consensus). Three structures recommended (bull put spread, diagonal calendar, risk reversal). Trader picked the **diagonal calendar**. Q1 print landed in textbook bull scenario — Q2 guide above consensus + a multi-hundred-million-dollar 2027 forward-order announcement (Reuters). Stock gapped +13.6% (vs implied ±10.8%), exceeding the diagonal's optimal pin range. Closed at open for **small profit (~5-15% of capital deployed)**. **Structural lesson: when directional conviction is high AND IV is event-driven, pick the directional defined-risk structure (bull put spread), not a pin-style calendar — the diagonal returned a small fraction of what the alternatives would have.**

## Strategy Evolution

### Stage 1: Three structures recommended (5/12 intraday)

Pre-earnings analysis identified high IV + event-driven IV term + directional bull conviction. Three defined-risk structures offered:

| Structure | Entry | Net delta | Net vega | Bull case P/L |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Bull put spread 5/15 $200/$185** | $3.00 credit | + | − (short vega) | ~97% of max |
| **Diagonal calendar 5/15-6/18 $230C** | $7.00 debit | small + | + (long vega) | small profit only |
| **Risk reversal 5/15 $200P / $235C** | $1.00 credit | strong + | mixed | large multi-x return |

### Stage 2: Trader picked diagonal (5/12 close)

**Trade entered**:
- SELL 5/15 $230 CALL @ ~$4.50 credit (IV 178%)
- BUY 6/18 $230 CALL @ ~$11.50 debit (IV 95%)
- Net debit: **$7.00 / 合约** (max risk $700)

**Rationale at time of entry**:
- Capture IV crush on front-week (173% → ~85% post-print)
- Retain long-vega exposure on back-month for post-print follow-through
- Long delta exposure (small but positive)
- Net debit limits downside

**Flaw in hindsight**: The structure assumes stock pins near $230. With implied move ±$23.5 and strike $12 OTM from spot, the **expected move distance was already 2x the strike-to-spot distance**. Any directional resolution would push stock past the short strike and degrade the structure to delta-neutral.

### Stage 4: Close at open (5/13 9:30 ET)

Diagonal valuation at open ($250.55):

| Leg | Entry | Open value | Per-leg P/L |
|---|---|---|---|
| Short 5/15 $230C | +$4.50 credit | ~$22 (intrinsic $20.55 + ~$1.5 residual) | −$17.5 |
| Long 6/18 $230C | −$11.50 debit | ~$28-30 (intrinsic $20.55 + extrinsic $7-9) | +$16.5 to +$18.5 |
| **Spread net** | −$7.00 | ~$7.5-9 | **small profit / 合约** |

Both legs went deep ITM, net deltas neutralized (each ~0.95), structure degraded to "intrinsic differential + long extrinsic minus short extrinsic." Profit came almost entirely from the long-leg extrinsic surviving event-driven IV crush.

**Closed both legs at open**. Net result: small profit, roughly **+5-15% of capital deployed**.

## What Worked

1. **Pre-earnings analysis identified bull scenario correctly** with concrete trigger criteria (Q2 guide above consensus + concrete forward-order announcement)
2. **Defined-risk discipline** — max loss $700/合约 was bounded; even worst case wouldn't have killed account
3. **Closed at open instead of holding** — gamma risk on short 5/15 $230C with 2 DTE was real; correct decision to take profit immediately
4. **Used proprietary directional conviction as the primary anchor** rather than Street consensus — this is what made the direction call accurate

## What Went Wrong (Structure Selection)

1. **Picked wrong axis weighting**: Diagonal calendar is a *pin* structure (best when stock stays near strike) and a *vega* play (long back-month vega). The pre-earnings setup gave **strong directional conviction** — the right structure is one that maximizes delta capture, not vega.

2. **Strike placement was the second error**: $230 strike was only $12.36 OTM (5.7%) vs implied move ±$23.5 (±10.8%). For a calendar to work, expected move should be **less than strike-to-spot distance**, not the opposite. The structure was set up to fail directionally even if the trader had picked the right level.

3. **Anchored on "IV crush" as the dominant edge** when the primary edge was actually "Street estimate revision wave from the forward-order announcement". IV crush is a small dollar-amount edge; a meaningful EPS-gap closing is a large dollar-amount edge. **Trade the bigger edge.**

4. **Failed to do the simple counterfactual**: had the trader simulated "what does each structure return if Bull case prints?" — bull put spread returns ~97% max, risk reversal returns a multi-x return, diagonal returns ~5-15%. The diagonal **caps the upside** in the very scenario the trader expected.

## Lessons / Updates to Framework

### Structure-selection rule when directional conviction is high

When proprietary research / internal model implies a meaningful price gap to sell-side consensus on forward EPS, the resulting trade has **high directional conviction**. Match structure accordingly:

| Conviction level | Structure family |
|---|---|
| High (large internal-vs-Street gap) | **Directional defined-risk**: bull put spread, risk reversal, vertical debit spread |
| Medium gap | Bull put spread or risk reversal; some calendar OK if IV is event-driven |
| Low gap (close to consensus) | Pin/vega plays acceptable (calendar, diagonal, iron condor) |

### Calendar/diagonal sanity check

Before entering a calendar/diagonal:
- **Implied move ÷ distance from spot to short strike ≤ 1.0**
- If ratio > 1.0, the structure is set up to fail directionally
- If ratio > 1.5 (this trade: 23.5/12 = 1.96), structure is essentially miscalibrated — pick directional instead

## Reusable Framework: High-Conviction Earnings Plays

Step-by-step framework when directional conviction (from any source — internal research, channel checks, sector setup) is high going into an event:

### 1. Quantify the conviction (mandatory pre-trade)
- How far is your directional view from sell-side consensus?
- What single data point on the print would force consensus to revise?
- Anchor probability that the print triggers a revision wave

### 2. Identify the catalyst structure
- For TSEM: it was forward order book commitment (large 2027 deal announcement)
- Build the bull case around "what single fact closes the gap"

### 3. Pick the structure on TWO axes
- **Axis A — Direction**: high-conviction bull → long delta
- **Axis B — Vega**: event-IV → short vega → credit structure preferred
- Default for "high directional conviction + high event-IV": **bull put spread or risk reversal**

### 4. Sanity-check strike placement
- For credit spreads: short strike outside ±0.5σ of implied move
- For debit spreads: long strike at or just OTM of expected move
- For calendars: do NOT enter unless implied move < strike distance × 0.75

### 5. Counterfactual P/L check
- Calculate P/L in Bull, Base, Bear scenarios for each candidate structure
- Pick the structure that captures the most upside in your highest-conviction scenario
- Bound max loss in worst case to ≤ planned position size

### 6. Exit discipline
- Bull case prints → close 80-90% of position at open, do NOT hold for last 10% (gamma risk)
- Base case prints → ride to mid-day, evaluate after conference call
- Bear case prints → take max loss immediately if defined-risk
