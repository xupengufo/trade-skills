# SNOW Q1 FY26 Earnings Trade Case Study (May 2026)

Pre-earnings setup had **5+ aligned bull factors** (6/6 Funda channel checks bullish, OpenAI/Anthropic as new top customers, Cortex Code AI narrative, stock pre-beaten -30% from highs, 4/5 prior positive earnings reactions). Recommendation given: **Jade Lizard 5/29 $155P / $195C / $200C, credit ~$4.50** — a capped-upside neutral-to-mildly-bullish structure justified by IV Rank 89% (event-driven). Actual outcome: revenue beat 5%, Cortex Code accelerating, AH gapped **+35.75% to $237.92** — a **+3.2 SD move**. Jade Lizard call spread fully ITM, max loss ~$50/contract (bounded by long $200C). Counterfactual long 5/29 $185C would have returned +560%. **Canonical lesson: high-conviction bull setups forbid capped-upside structures even when high IV makes "sell premium" look optimal — asymmetry is the third axis beyond direction and vega.**

## Strategy Evolution

### Stage 1: Pre-earnings analysis (5/27 intraday)

Probability split I assigned:
- Bull (beat + raised guide + Cortex Code direct): **30%** → target $190-200 (+9-15%)
- Base (beat + in-line guide): **40%** → target $172-188 (-1 to +8%)
- Bear (beat but weak guide): **30%** → target $150-165 (-5 to -14%)

**Where this was wrong**: I treated the 6/6 channel check confluence and the new-customer disclosure (OpenAI/Anthropic) as already-priced-in. They were not. The bull tail should have been weighted ≥ 50%, with non-trivial probability (15-20%) on a "blowout / multiple re-rate" tail beyond +15%.

### Stage 2: Structure recommendation

**Picked**: Jade Lizard 5/29 $155P / $195C / $200C, target credit $4.50
- Vega: short ✓ (matched IV Rank 89%)
- Direction: mildly bullish (acceptable on the direction axis)
- **Asymmetry: capped at $450/contract for ANY upside above $195** ❌

**Justified by**:
- IV 89th percentile → sell premium (pitfall 7) ✓ on vega axis
- Net credit ≥ call spread width → "zero upside risk" ← TRUE but misleading; zero risk ≠ zero opportunity cost
- 1-SD strangle 65-70% POP — sounded high
- Defined risk bounded loss

**Real flaw**: I optimized for "capture IV crush with defined risk" rather than for "max P&L when bull case prints." Counterfactual not run. Pitfall 6 (clever structures fading conviction) should have triggered — the Jade Lizard was clever-structure-for-its-own-sake when the underlying conviction warranted simple long delta.

### Stage 3: Print (5/27 AMC)

**Headline**:
- Revenue actual: **$1.391B** vs $1.324B consensus, vs Funda preview $1.366B
- Beat: **+5.07% vs Street, +1.8% vs Funda preview**
- YoY: ~**33.4%** (vs 27% consensus, 31% Funda preview)
- Cortex Code contribution + AI Labs as top customers explicitly confirmed
- Guide details (presumed strong based on AH reaction)

**AH price**: **$237.92** = **+35.75% from $175.26 close** = **+3.2 SD vs implied ±$19.80**

This is the very rare blowout tail. But the setup *predicted* it: 6 channel checks + AI narrative + new customer disclosure + beaten-down stock = the trifecta for a multi-week re-rate cycle (not just a one-day pop).

### Stage 4: Jade Lizard P&L (closed at T+1 open assumption)

| Leg | Entry credit | At $237.92 | Per-leg P/L |
|---|---|---|---|
| Short $155 Put | +$1.50 | ~$0 (worthless) | **+$1.50** |
| Short $195 Call | +$3.00 | ~$42.92 ITM | **-$39.92** |
| Long $200 Call | -$0.00 (net) | ~$37.92 ITM | **+$37.92** |
| Call spread net | net +$3.00 credit | -$5.00 max (width fully ITM) | **-$2.00** |
| **Total** | **+$4.50 credit** | call spread max + put kept | **~-$0.50/share = -$50/contract** |

**Max loss bounded at $50/contract** by the long $200C — this is the *only* good thing about the structure in this scenario. Without the protective long call, naked short $195C against $237 would have been -$42.92/share = -$4,292/contract.

### Counterfactual alternatives — what should have been recommended

| Structure | Entry cost/credit | P/L @ $237.92 | Multiple of Jade Lizard |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Naked Short $145P** (cash-secured) | +$2.50 credit | full credit kept = **+$250** | ~5x better |
| **Bull Put Spread $160/$145** | +$3.50 credit | full credit kept = **+$350** | ~7x better |
| **Long 5/29 $200C** (single) | -$3.00 debit | ~$37.92 = **+$3,500/contract** | ~70x better |
| **Long 5/29 $185C** (single) | -$8.00 debit | ~$52.92 = **+$4,500/contract** | ~90x better |

The naked short put and bull put spread were the **same vega sign** as the Jade Lizard but **uncapped on the upside leg** — they captured the IV crush + delta + nothing-to-give-back. The long calls accepted the vega tax but got paid massively for the tail. Either family is acceptable; only Jade Lizard / IC / Calendar / Diagonal-with-tight-strikes is forbidden in this regime.

## What I Got Wrong

1. **Under-weighted confluence factor**. Pitfall 14 says discount a single channel check by 30-40%. But 6 aligned independent channel checks across NA + Europe are **not** N=6 noisy samples — they are confluence evidence. The confluence multiplier should INCREASE conviction, not be discounted as "selection bias." Treat aligned-multi-source signals as strong, not as N-times-an-unreliable-signal.

2. **Treated NEW information as priced-in**. OpenAI and Anthropic as top customers was disclosed in the Funda preview, but Funda preview ≠ market knowledge. Most buy-side knew about Cortex Code; only the most informed knew about AI Labs scale. This was the kind of disclosure that triggers **multiple repricing**, not just earnings beat.

3. **Pitfall 6 fired but I ignored it**. The Jade Lizard is a "clever structure" — it requires the trade to land in a precise corridor. When direction is genuinely high-conviction, simple long delta is the right answer. Picking the clever structure was masking that I wanted bull exposure but was afraid to commit delta.

4. **Counterfactual P&L matrix never computed**. The TSEM case study (one trade prior) had taught the exact same lesson: "calculate P/L in Bull, Base, Bear scenarios for each candidate structure." I didn't do it for SNOW. If I had, the Jade Lizard's max-$450 vs long-call's potential +$4,500 would have been visible immediately.

5. **"High IV → sell premium" treated as the dominant edge**. Pitfall 7 is a vega-axis rule, not an asymmetry-axis rule. Within the set of short-vega structures, there are ones with great asymmetry (naked short put, bull put spread) and ones with terrible asymmetry (Jade Lizard, IC). I picked the wrong one.

6. **Mental anchor on 2025-12-03 guide-miss case**. I let one prior negative reaction (-9.59%) inflate the bear-case weight to 30%, when 4 of 5 had been positive and the current setup looked more like the August 2025 (+24%) blowout regime. Recency bias toward a single negative print.

7. **Channel check growth math under-extrapolated**. Funda partners gave growth ranges like "+28-29%", "+30-35% in 2Q", but the Funda preview's final 31% estimate was the conservative midpoint. The Europe partner said 39% in 1Q26 and 42% FY26. I should have considered that consensus + Funda preview + actual could be a chain where actual > Funda > consensus — and that's exactly what happened.

## Lessons / Updates to Framework

### New pitfall added

`../pitfalls/24-capped-upside-vs-bull-conviction.md` — Capped-upside structures are forbidden in high-conviction bull setups. Asymmetry is a third axis beyond direction and vega.

### Updated rule in strategies.md

Add: **Asymmetry / upside profile check** — after direction and vega axes match, calculate "max profit if bull tail (+20%, +35%, +50%) prints" for each candidate structure. Reject any structure that flat-lines or loses in the bull tail when bull-conviction count ≥ 4.

### Confluence vs sample-bias clarification

Pitfall 14 (single channel check is a sample) is correct, but it's about **single-source over-extrapolation**. Add complementary rule: **N≥3 INDEPENDENT channel checks all aligned in the same direction is confluence evidence, not N-times sample bias**. The right discount is on uncorrelated samples, not on confirmatory cross-validation.

## Reusable Framework: High-Conviction Bull Earnings Plays

### 1. Run the bull-conviction count

Mandatory before structure selection. Each = 1 point:

- [ ] 3+ independent channel checks aligned bullish
- [ ] Sector / thematic narrative actively re-rating
- [ ] Stock down >20% from recent high (de-risked setup)
- [ ] Past 4 quarters: ≥3 positive earnings reactions
- [ ] NEW information likely to be disclosed (new customer tier, new product class, guide raise)
- [ ] Net options flow back-month bullish (call premium dominance)
- [ ] Short interest >10%
- [ ] Implied move materially below recent realized average

**Count ≥ 4 = high-conviction bull. Asymmetry rule activates.**

SNOW score: 6/8 ✓ (channel checks, AI narrative, de-risked, prior earnings reactions, new customer info, implied move at recent avg) — should have triggered the rule immediately.

### 2. Filter structures by asymmetry

Banned in high-conviction bull (≥ 4 conviction points):
- ❌ Jade Lizard
- ❌ Iron Condor
- ❌ Calendar / Diagonal with tight strikes
- ❌ Covered Call against new entry
- ❌ Bear Call spread + Short Put combo (any form of capped upside)

Allowed:
- ✓ Naked short put (cash-secured)
- ✓ Bull put spread
- ✓ Risk reversal (sell put + buy call)
- ✓ Synthetic long
- ✓ Long call (if IV is willing to pay)
- ✓ Stock + protective put

### 3. Vega-axis filter

Among allowed structures, prefer short-vega ones when IV Rank > 70:
- Best: Naked short put or bull put spread far OTM
- Acceptable: Risk reversal (mixed vega, captures upside)
- Last resort: Long call (vega tax acceptable if implied move is small vs actual setup)

### 4. Run the counterfactual matrix

For each candidate structure, compute P/L at: spot, +10%, +20%, +35%, +50%. If any "bull tail" column shows flat or loss, REJECT the structure.

### 5. Position sizing

- 1-2% of capital per single-name event (defined-risk version)
- If using long calls, smaller (0.5-1%) — accepting vega tax means each contract has lower probability win
- Combine 70% bull put spread (vol clipper) + 30% long calls (tail catcher) for blend

### 6. Exit discipline

- Bull case prints (T+0 AH): close at T+1 open, lock max profit on credit structures
- Long calls: scale out 50% at T+1 open, hold 50% for post-earnings drift T+2 to T+5 (pitfall 20)
- If wrong direction: defined-risk structures cap loss; no panic action needed
