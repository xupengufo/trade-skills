# INTC Earnings Trade Case Study (April 2026)

Real trade arc showing how thesis, structure, and decisions evolved over 4 days around the Q1 2026 earnings release. Net swing from flipping bear → bull at thesis invalidation: **+$3.78**.

---

## Setup

- **Ticker**: INTC
- **Entry window**: Apr 20 (Monday) — Apr 24 (Friday expiry)
- **Earnings**: Apr 23 (Thursday) after market close
- **Starting context**:
  - Stock YTD +77% ($18 → $66)
  - IV Rank 88% (1-year high)
  - Analyst consensus had moved $47 → $55 → $66 in 3 months (trailing)
  - 52w high $70.33 (failed retest)

---

## Strategy Evolution

### Stage 1: Bear Put Spread (Apr 20)

**Thesis**: "Sell the news" — stock at consensus, high IV, failed breakout = exhaustion.

**Structure**: 5/1 $65/$58 bear put spread.
- Debit: ~$2.56
- Max profit: $4.44 / Max loss: $2.56
- Breakeven: $62.44

**Flaw in hindsight**: Anchored on "price = consensus $66" as ceiling. Ignored that consensus was *rising* (analysts chasing, not capping).

---

### Stage 2: Put Diagonal (Apr 21)

**Trigger for switch**: Discovered extreme IV term skew.

| Expiry | IV |
|--------|-----|
| 4/24 (earnings week) | 121% |
| 5/1 | 89% |
| 5/15 | 73% |

**New structure**: Long 5/15 $66P + Short 4/24 $64P
- Net debit: $2.65
- Idea: sell expensive front-week premium, own cheaper back-month

**Hidden signal**: The "tactical upgrade" from vanilla bear spread to diagonal was partially masking declining conviction in the bearish direction. Clever structures can hide uncertainty.

---

### Stage 3: Bull Put Spread Flip (Apr 23, pre-earnings)

**Trigger for reversal**: 
- Tesla-Intel 14A news (Apr 22 AH) — new bullish catalyst
- Stock held above $67 all day on Apr 23 — no fade
- Volume profile weakening but price action firm
- Sell-the-news *preconditions* (weak tape, volume dropoff) failed

**New structure**: Closed diagonal at scratch, opened 4/24 $66/$62 bull put spread
- Credit: $1.35
- Max profit: $1.35 (if INTC ≥ $66 at expiry)
- Max loss: $2.65

**P&L**:

---

## Key Lessons From This Trade

### What went wrong in early analysis
1. Treated "price at consensus" as bearish — consensus was trailing, not predictive
2. Overweighted a single $837k institutional LEAP call seller as "smart money" — that seller ended up $2.5M underwater
3. Started with DCF ($40-48 fair value) instead of tape — fought momentum
4. Overreacted to Tesla news (+2.7% AH) — user correctly pushed back that it was ~70% priced in already

### What went right
1. **Always used defined-risk structures** — max loss bounded throughout
2. **Willing to flip thesis when tape invalidated it** — the single most valuable skill demonstrated
3. **Correct structure at the right time**: high-IV + bullish → credit spread (bull put), not debit
4. **Exited at scratch when EV turned negative** — didn't cling to dead thesis

### Meta-insight
The evolution bear spread → diagonal → bull put was driven by **three different questions**:
- Bear spread: "What should I do?" (directional)
- Diagonal: "How should I structure it?" (tactical)
- Bull put: "Why am I doing this?" (philosophical)

Each stage corresponded to a higher-order question. **Most traders never reach Stage 3** — they anchor on Stage 1 thesis and die defending it.
