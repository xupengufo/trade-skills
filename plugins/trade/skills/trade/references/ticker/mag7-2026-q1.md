# Mag-7 Q1 2026 Earnings Cluster — Trade Framework Notes

Notes from the Mag-7 cluster earnings event of 2026-04-29 (MSFT / GOOGL / AMZN reporting same night). Three different IV/duration regimes matched to three different structures. Two of three direction calls correct; portfolio net positive due to multi-name diversification.

---

## Setup

- **Tickers**: MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN (META intentionally excluded after directional review)
- **Earnings date**: 2026-04-29 AMC (3 names same night)
- **Pre-existing exposure**: Long-dated MSFT bullish position held into the event
- **Macro overlay**: Fed FOMC same day, Iran/Hormuz tensions, "OpenAI warning" (WSJ) on 4/28-29 hitting AI capex sentiment

### Pre-earnings IV / move state

| Ticker | IVR | Front-week ATM IV | Implied Move |
|--------|-----|-------------------|--------------|
| MSFT | ~100% (1Y peak) | ~117% | ±7.4% |
| GOOGL | ~54% (low for earnings) | ~44% | ±5.6% |
| AMZN | ~63% | ~116% | ±7.3% |

**Asymmetry**: GOOGL's far lower IV vs the two AI-capex-heavy names was the cleanest tell — options market saw GOOGL as least binary.

## Strategy Composition

Three different structures matched to three different IV/conviction states:

| Name | IV state | Conviction | Structure type | Rationale |
|------|----------|------------|----------------|-----------|
| MSFT | High IVR + LEAPS held | Multi-quarter bull | Diagonal (long LEAPS + short front-week call) | Collect front-week vega/theta to offset LEAPS IV crush; cap on upside accepted |
| GOOGL | Low IVR + tape strong | Single-quarter bull | Bull call debit spread (back-month) | Low IV makes debit cheap; back-month avoids front-week IV peak; vega ~neutral within spread |
| AMZN | Mid IVR + tape recovering | Single-quarter bull | Bull put credit spread (front-week) | Sell elevated front-week IV; benefit from IV crush; defined risk |

The portfolio was **structurally diversified across three different vol/duration regimes**, not just "three bullish bets."

## What the Framework Got Right

1. **Structure-to-regime matching** worked. GOOGL low-IV → debit; MSFT high-IV LEAPS → diagonal; AMZN mid-IV → credit. Each structure captured the regime it was designed for.

2. **Excluding META** was correct. Reduced exposure to a known capex-shock candidate.

3. **Defined-risk on every leg**. No naked positions. Maximum drawdown bounded.

4. **3-name diversification absorbed single-thesis failure**. The MSFT directional thesis missed but the portfolio still ended net positive — this is the textbook value of multi-name structure.

5. **Discipline on stop-loss**. The user cut the LEAPS leg when MSFT broke below the BE region instead of holding for the multi-quarter thesis. The stock did briefly drift slightly lower before bouncing — the cut was near the bottom, validating the discipline.

6. **Two of three direction calls correct**. GOOGL beat → spread to max. AMZN beat → credit kept near full.

---

## What the Framework Got Wrong

### 1. Preconditions met ≠ stock direction

The MSFT lesson: every fundamental data point hit or exceeded the precondition threshold (segment growth, AI ARR, paid-user counts, EPS, cost discipline signals). Stock still went down materially over T+0 AH and T+1 day.

**Why**: We confused "thesis correct" with "stock will rally." Sentiment + forward guidance + sector mood are independent variables that can override fundamentals in the short term.

**Fix**: Add **sentiment preconditions** alongside fundamental ones:
- Sector mood (e.g., "AI cohort not -2%+ on earnings day")
- Forward guidance (e.g., "next-quarter segment growth ≥ X%")
- Buy-side flow (e.g., "if buy-side reports widely circulated, raise threshold by 10-20%")

### 2. AH initial reaction did not predict next-day open

MSFT initial AH dipped sharply, recovered to nearly flat by 21:00 EST, then gapped down again the next morning and drifted further intraday before bouncing.

**Why**: 16:00-20:00 AH is still price discovery. Sell-side notes drop overnight. Rating actions hit pre-market. T+1 morning is closer to "real" price than T+0 evening.

**Fix**: Default exit/reload decisions to T+1 morning, not T+0 evening, unless the position is already at structural max profit (in which case lock at T+0 AH).

### 3. Buy-side report flow inflated the bar

Multiple buy-side previews circulated pre-earnings with elevated estimates relative to sell-side consensus. When the company printed in-line with buy-side (well above sell-side), the market interpreted it as "bare meet" rather than "beat."

**Fix**: When buy-side reports become widely cited, treat their thresholds as the *minimum* expectation, not stretch goals. Add safety margin above buy-side levels.

### 4. LEAPS through earnings = unhedged vega tax

Long-dated long calls have meaningful vega exposure. Earnings IV crush represents a guaranteed loss component independent of direction. Holding a LEAPS through earnings without hedging is implicitly a short-vol bet.

**Fix**:
- Calculate vega tax explicitly: `vega × 100 × estimated IV crush in pp`
- Choose one of: (a) buy short-dated put hedge, (b) convert to diagonal, (c) close pre-earnings and re-enter post-IV-crush, (d) accept the bleed in sizing

### 5. Sector mood overrode individual merits

The "OpenAI warning" narrative on 4/28-29 hit the AI capex cohort broadly. Even names with strong individual fundamentals faced selling pressure on capex/ROI concerns. Pre-earnings positioning (-1% the day-of) was a *leading* indicator, not a contrarian one.

**Fix**: When sector cohort sells off pre-earnings on a sector narrative, that narrative will likely dominate the post-earnings reaction even if individual fundamentals are strong. Hedge sector beta separately.

### 6. User intuition occasionally beat the framework

Pre-earnings, the directional intuition that "capex must increase, therefore the stock will sell off on ROI fears" was correct. The framework's response was "depends on whether revenue justifies it" — technically true but operationally less useful than the simpler intuition.

**Fix**: When user intuition diverges from framework, treat the divergence as a signal that the framework is missing a sector-level layer. Don't override intuition without explicit reasoning.

## Meta-Insight: Three Layers of Correctness

1. **Fundamentals correct** ✅ — earnings data hit thresholds
2. **Direction correct** ❌ — one of three names fell despite hitting thresholds
3. **P&L outcome correct** ✅ — diversification absorbed the single failure

The trade was **structurally right** even when one **directional call was wrong**. This is the value of multi-leg, multi-name options portfolios: any single thesis can fail, but structure + diversification can preserve positive expectancy.

---

## Patterns to Watch on Future Mag-7 Earnings Clusters

1. **Same-night cluster reactions are correlated by sector mood**. If 1 name reports a "capex shock" early, expect the others to drag even on good prints.
2. **AH 21:00 price ≠ next-day open**. Plan for a 2-day window.
3. **Front-week IV 100%+ regimes** favor selling premium structures over buying.
4. **Back-month spreads** (5/15+) in low-IV names sidestep the worst of the front-week IV crush bog.
5. **Buy-side preview proliferation** = market expectation creep. Recalibrate thresholds upward.
6. **Capex-sensitive names** (heavy AI infra spend) are increasingly subject to ROI panic episodes that override fundamental beats. Hedge sector beta separately.
7. **LEAPS holders should always evaluate hedge structures** before earnings — vega tax is a guaranteed bleed otherwise.

---

## Updated Trading Rules Adopted Post-Event

1. Track both **fundamental preconditions** AND **sentiment preconditions** before earnings.
2. Plan for a **2-day window** post-earnings; T+1 morning is the real price.
3. **Buy-side reports** become consensus when widely cited — recalibrate thresholds upward.
4. **LEAPS through earnings** require explicit hedge analysis or accepted vega bleed.
5. **Sector mood** can dominate individual merits in short term — separate hedge.
6. **User intuition divergence** from framework is a signal worth weighting, not overriding.
7. **Discipline > thesis loyalty**. Cutting at structural BE is correct even when multi-quarter thesis is intact.
8. **Multi-name diversification** is the portfolio-level safety net for single-name thesis failure.
