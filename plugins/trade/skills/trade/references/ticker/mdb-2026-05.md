# MDB Q1 FY27 Earnings Trade Case Study (May 2026)

The day **after** SNOW gapped +35.75% (2026-05-27, the canonical Jade-Lizard-capped-upside failure), MDB reported into the *same* red-hot AI-database theme. Surface pattern said "SNOW repeat → high-conviction bull → buy uncapped upside." The framework said otherwise: MDB's bull-conviction count was a **mechanical ~4/8 but low-quality** — the stock was **+10.5% intraday into the print (chasing, not de-risked)**, today's **flow was put-premium-heavy (protective, not bullish)**, and the **most recent print (Mar 2026) had crashed −22.2% from the *exact same* $325 level**. Verdict: not a high-conviction bull, but a **high-IV + mild-bull + real-fade-risk** setup. Recommendation: **5/29 bull put spread $285/$265 (~$4.50 credit) as the workhorse + a small 6/18 long-call tail; explicitly REJECT Iron Condor / Jade Lizard (don't sell the freshly-demonstrated upside tail) and naked puts (March −22% precedent).** Outcome: EPS $1.32 vs $1.19 beat, stock gapped to $350.50, whipsawed $352.59 → $303.97, closed **$335.55 (+3.0% day / +10% two-day)** — a moderate, violently choppy move that landed squarely in the base/mild-bull zone. **Bull put spread expired at max profit (~99%); the small long-call tail made only a few hundred (no blowout, vega tax ate most of the directional gain). Net: clean win, and a validation that the bull-conviction count must weight factor *quality*, not just count.**

## Strategy Evolution

### Stage 1: Pattern-match to SNOW (5/28 intraday, $325)

**Surface read**: SNOW +35.75% yesterday on AI-database/Cortex; MDB is the same theme; channel checks bullish; serial beater → "high-conviction bull, buy uncapped upside (long calls / risk reversal), the SNOW playbook."

**Why this was resisted**: ran the bull-conviction count *and weighted factor quality* rather than counting mechanically.

Plus the unscored killer: **MDB sat at the exact $325 level it crashed −22.2% from one quarter prior** (**Inversion #3**, priced-in / overhead memory).

**Verdict**: SNOW was a **6/8 high-quality** bull → asymmetry rule fired hard → uncapped structures, long calls the hero. MDB was a **~4/8 but low-quality** count with 3 inversions → **NOT a high-conviction bull**. Reclassified as **high-IV + mild-bull lean + real fade-risk + fat tails both ways** (the +38% Aug tail AND the −22% Mar tail were both live).

### Stage 3: Three-axis structure decision

- **Vega = short, but edge over-stated.** IVR 100 says sell premium, but realized (~24%) > implied (±14%) means the skew already prices the crash risk fairly → **defined risk mandatory**, no naked.
- **Direction = mild bull, real fade.** +10.5% pre-run priced in much of the easy move; put-heavy flow; March crash from this level.
- **Asymmetry = both tails fat.** Can't sell calls (SNOW +35.75% *yesterday* + MDB's own +38% Aug); can't sell naked puts (−22% Mar). → The only defensible short-premium expression is a **bull put spread** + an optional **small long-call tail** (in the 107% back-month, NOT the 390% front).

**Recommended**:
- **Core: 5/29 bull put spread $285/$265, ~$4.50 credit** (short $285 *below* the $294 pre-run close → survives a full give-back of the day's rip), max loss ~$15.50, BE $280.50, ~62% POP.
- **Optional tail (≤1%): 6/18 $370/$400 call debit (~$7)** or small single 6/18 call — blowout-catcher using 107% IV, not 390%.
- **REJECTED**: Iron Condor / Jade Lizard (would sell the fat, freshly-demonstrated upside tail — pitfall 24); naked / cash-secured put (March −22%); long 5/29 premium (390–430% vega tax).

### Stage 4: Print + path (5/28 AMC → 5/29 expiry)

- **Headline**: EPS **$1.32** vs $1.19 (+11%); Rev **$687.6M** vs $664.5M (+3.5%). Another solid beat — exactly as the serial-beater history implied.
- **Path**: 5/29 **open $350.50 (+7.6% gap)** → **high $352.59** → **dumped to low $303.97 (−6.7% from open base)** → **close $335.55**.

## What Worked

1. **Refused the SNOW pattern-match on quality, not count.** The mechanical ~4/8 looked bullish; the 3 inversions (chasing not de-risked, put-heavy flow, same-level −22% crash precedent) correctly reclassified it as *mixed-conviction*. The moderate, choppy outcome (+3%/+10%, not +35%) validated NOT chasing with big long-call size.
2. **Bull put spread as the workhorse.** It harvested the 390% front-week IV crush, profited across the **entire** non-crash range ($285→∞), and the cushion ($285 below the $294 pre-run close) was vindicated when MDB whipsawed to $303.97 intraday. 99% of max with zero stress.
3. **Sized the long calls small — correct on process AND outcome.** No blowout → calls barely profited (the +10% two-day move couldn't drag OTM strikes ITM, and IV crush ate the rest). Had they been the *main* bet (SNOW-style), they'd have badly underperformed the SP. In a mixed-conviction setup, long calls are a tail-hedge you're happy to see expire near-flat — not the hero.
4. **Defined risk over naked.** The intraday dump to $303.97 (and the live −22% March precedent) is exactly why naked puts were banned. The spread shrugged off a $49 intraday swing.
5. **"Implied < realized → defined risk" was validated in real time.** The stock made a ~15% intraday peak-to-trough swing; a too-tight or naked structure would have been whipsawed. The cushion absorbed it.

## What I Got Wrong (Analyst Side)

1. **The long-call tail added complexity for ~nothing.** For a genuinely mixed-conviction setup, the cleanest expression was *just the bull put spread*. The call tail was defensible insurance, but the honest read is the SP alone was the trade; the calls were a small concession to FOMO about a SNOW-repeat.
2. **Under-sized the core relative to its robustness.** Ex-post, the bull put spread was never within $19 of its short strike. A slightly more aggressive $300/$280 ($6.50 credit) would have made +$650 and *still* survived (low $303.97 > $300 by $4). The $285 strike left credit on the table — though that's hindsight; coming within $4 of $300 intraday shows $285 was the prudent call, not an error.
3. **Did not forecast the gap-up→spike→dump→recover whipsaw path.** Called the *magnitude* (moderate) and *defined-risk need* correctly, but the path was wilder than framed. Anyone who chased long calls at the $350.50 open or $352.59 high got dragged to $303.97 before the recovery — a live demonstration of the priced-in/fade risk the +10.5% pre-run created.
4. **Probability tilt (bear 40 / bull 35 / base 25) over-weighted the clean bear tail.** The −22% March repeat didn't print — but the intraday dump to $303.97 showed the fade risk was real and merely *resolved* up. Path-right, endpoint-off; the structure was robust either way.

## Lessons / Updates to Framework

### 1. Bull-conviction count: weight factor QUALITY, not just the mechanical count (refines pitfall 24)

SNOW (6/8 **coherent**) and MDB (~4/8 with **3 inversions**) were one day apart in the same theme. A mechanical tally would have flagged both as "approaching high-conviction bull." The discriminator was **coherence**: a count near threshold but riddled with inversions (chasing-not-de-risked, flow-against, recent-same-level-crash) is **mixed-conviction**, not high-conviction. The asymmetry rule's *ban on capped-upside* still applies (the upside tail was fat), but the *prescription to buy uncapped upside* does NOT — the workhorse is the bull put spread, with long calls demoted to a small tail.

→ Proposed addition to the bull-conviction checklist in `../strategies.md` and pitfall 24: **score factor quality; ≥2 inversions among the core factors (not de-risked / flow-against-thesis / adverse recent-print at same level) downgrades a borderline count from "high-conviction bull" to "mixed-conviction → bull put spread workhorse, long calls small."**

### 2. The "mixed-conviction middle zone" structure map

Between SNOW (high-conviction bull → long calls / risk reversal) and a pure neutral (Iron Condor) sits the **high-IV + mild-bull + fade-risk** zone:
- **Workhorse**: bull put spread (short the rich front IV, mild bull, no upside cap-LOSS, defined downside).
- **Tail**: small long call in the *back month* (avoid front-week vega tax), sized to expire near-flat without regret.
- **Banned**: Iron Condor / Jade Lizard (fat upside tail), naked puts (fat downside tail), long front-week premium (vega tax).

### 3. "Don't sell a freshly-demonstrated fat tail" — process beats outcome

MDB closed $335.55, high only $352.59 — so an Iron Condor's short-call side (≥$380) would have survived and the IC would have made *marginally more* this time. **This does not make selling the upside correct.** SNOW had gone +35.75% the literal day before; selling MDB's upside tail 24h later for ~$2 credit is picking up pennies in front of a steamroller that had just run over the comparable name. The intraday spike to $352.59 shows how easily it could have kept going. **Refusing the IC was correct risk management even though the tail didn't print — and it cost nothing, because the bull put spread captured max profit anyway.** This is the SNOW lesson (pitfall 24) applied *correctly* on the next occurrence.

### 4. Validations (no new pitfall, reinforced existing)

- Pitfall 9 (preconditions ≠ direction): beat was certain; the reaction was a coin-flip-with-fat-tails that resolved moderate-up after a violent chop.
- Pitfall 5/8 (priced-in is a percentage): the +10.5% pre-run priced in the easy money; post-print was chop, not clean continuation.
- Pitfall 21 (event-IV vs demand-IV): 3.6× term skew + IVR 100 = event-driven; the front-week crush was the guaranteed edge the SP harvested.
- Pitfall 7 (IV crush favors short premium): the dominant, near-certain edge — captured by the SP, paid-for by the long call (vega tax).

## Reusable Framework: High-IV + Mixed-Conviction Earnings Plays (the SNOW-adjacent "looks like a blowout, isn't" setup)

### 1. Run the bull-conviction count — then audit it for inversions
Tally the 8 factors. **Then check for inversions among the core three:**
- Is the stock *chasing* (up hard into the print) rather than *de-risked* (beaten down)?
- Is today's *flow against* the thesis (put-premium-heavy on an up day)?
- Is there an *adverse recent print at the same price level* (memory/overhead)?

**≥2 inversions → downgrade to mixed-conviction**, even if the raw count ≥4.

### 2. Map to the structure zone
- **High-conviction bull (coherent ≥4, no inversions)** → long calls / risk reversal / bull put spread (SNOW).
- **Mixed-conviction (count high, ≥2 inversions)** → **bull put spread workhorse + small back-month long-call tail** (MDB). ← this case
- **Genuine neutral (no directional edge, no fat tails)** → Iron Condor.
- Banned in the first two: selling the upside (IC/JL), naked the fat tail, long front-week premium.

### 3. Place the short put with give-back cushion
Put the short strike **below the pre-run prior close**, so a full give-back of the run-up day doesn't breach it. (MDB: $285 short vs $294 pre-run close → survived a whipsaw to $303.97.)

### 4. Size the long-call tail to be forgettable
≤1% of capital, back-month IV not front-week. In a moderate move it makes "a few hundred" and that's the *correct* outcome — it's blowout insurance, not the thesis. If you'd be upset seeing it expire near-flat, you've mis-sized it.

### 5. Counterfactual matrix before submitting (mandatory)
Compute P/L at −22% / −10% / flat / +10% / +20% / +38%. Confirm the core (bull put spread) is flat-positive across the whole up-range and the only loss is a deep crash; confirm you did NOT add a structure (IC/JL) that loses in the +20%/+38% columns.

### 6. Exit
1-DTE core: let it ride to expiry; defined risk means no panic on an intraday dump (MDB dumped to $303.97 and still paid max). Long-call tail: scale or let expire; don't chase the AH high.
