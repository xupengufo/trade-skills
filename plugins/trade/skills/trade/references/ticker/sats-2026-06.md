# SATS (EchoStar) SpaceX/AT&T Spectrum-Sale Case Study (June 2026)

Analysis of whether to buy $SATS as a "discounted SpaceX proxy" (thesis sourced from a Le Shrub substack post). Over the thread I made a **material, confident error**: I asserted EchoStar's SpaceX consideration was **dollar-anchored** (a fixed ~$11B of value, so "SpaceX's IPO pop barely flows through" and the bull's "+69% / ~$42B stake" was a *phantom*). The user pushed back with a piece of tape — *SATS rose every time SpaceX raised its pre-IPO price* — and on verification from EchoStar's **Nov 6 2025 8-K** the consideration is **share-anchored**: up to **$11.117B of SpaceX Class A stock at a FIXED $212/share** = a fixed share count ≈ **~2% of SpaceX**, which **marks to market**. SpaceX re-rated from ~$550B (signing) to ~**$2.52T** (post-IPO), so that stake is **~$50B today**, and SpaceX upside flows through **~1:1**. I had it backwards. **Canonical lesson: for any stock-based deal consideration, verify share-anchored vs dollar-anchored — and the split basis — from the primary agreement BEFORE concluding whether the acquirer's stock moves flow through. A fixed reference price ("valued at $212/sh") means a fixed share count, not a fixed dollar value. The market tape was the tell I ignored.**

## Analysis Evolution

### Stage 1 — Imported the bull thesis, correctly flagged its surface errors
Caught real problems in Le Shrub's bridge: it ignored the **going concern + skipped coupon**, understated net debt (used −$20.6B vs actual ~$27.9B), and was generous on the "remaining business." Good so far. **But I then over-extended into the consideration mechanics without verifying them.**

### Stage 2 — THE ERROR: asserted "dollar-anchored, no flow-through"
I read the 10-Q line — *"up to $8.5 billion will be paid in SpaceX's Class A Common Stock, **valued at $212 per share**"* — and concluded it was a **fixed $ value** (~$11B), so:
- "SpaceX's +40% pop barely flows to SATS."
- "Le Shrub's ~$42B SpaceX stake **doesn't exist** / double-counts."
- Verdict: "SATS is **not** a SpaceX proxy; it's a going-concern de-lever story; fair-to-rich at $118."

I even argued "**$192.50 < $212 deal price ⇒ the stake is slightly underwater**" — **mixing a post-split market price against a pre-split reference** (see Stage 4). And I had quoted SpaceX at "$192.44" **derived from Le Shrub's '+20% close,' not pulled live** (it later checked out, but I shouldn't have asserted it unsourced).

### Stage 3 — The pushback (the tell I'd ignored)
User: *"应该不是美元对价锁定的 — spcx 上市之前，每次上调上市价，sats 都会涨"* ("it shouldn't be dollar-locked — before SPCX listed, every time it raised the IPO price, SATS rose"). **This is a revealed-preference proof: if the consideration were a fixed $ value, SATS would not track SpaceX's valuation. It did → share-anchored.** The market had been telling me the mechanism the whole time.

### Stage 4 — Verified from the primary filing (Nov 6 2025 8-K)
> *"…the total consideration for the Transactions has increased from $17 billion to **$19,616,737,853**, with **up to $11,116,737,853 to be paid in [SpaceX's] Class A Common Stock, valued at $212 per share.**"*

- **$11.117B ÷ FIXED $212/sh = ~52.44M shares (pre-split)** — a **fixed share count**, locked at signing. The shares then float. This is **share-anchored**, not dollar-anchored.
- Corroborated by the 10-K risk factor: EchoStar will hold *"a **minority, non-controlling position**"* in SpaceX whose *"**valuation … would remain inherently uncertain**"* — i.e. an equity stake it **marks to market**, not a fixed receivable.

### Stage 5 — The split-basis error (mine and Le Shrub's, same bug inverted)
SpaceX did a **1:5 split** (user-supplied; corroborated by arithmetic — see below). So:
- The **$212 reference is pre-split**; the current **$192.50 is post-split**. My "$192.50 < $212 ⇒ underwater" compared **two different bases** — wrong. Same-basis, $192.50 post-split ≈ $962.50 pre-split, far **above** $212.
- EchoStar's **52.44M pre-split shares → ~262M post-split** (×5) — which is exactly **Le Shrub's "261.8M."** His "$42B" used **post-split share count (261.8M) × a pre-split price ($160)** — the same basis mix, inverted. His share *count* was right; his only error was the price basis. **He was directionally correct and I wrongly called the stake a phantom.**

**Arithmetic forcing the split**: if $212 and $192.50 shared a basis, signing valuation = 13.1B shares × $212 = $2.77T — impossible (SpaceX IPO'd at ~$1.77T in June 2026). So $212 must be on a smaller (pre-split) share base ≈ 2.6B shares → signing valuation ≈ **$551B**, a plausible Sept/Nov-2025 SpaceX private mark.

## Outcome (the corrected picture)

**EchoStar's SpaceX stake (split-invariant, via ownership %)**:

$$\text{% of SpaceX} = \frac{\$11.117\text{B}}{\sim\$551\text{B signing val}} \approx \mathbf{2.0\%}; \quad 2.0\% \times \$2.52\text{T} \approx \boxed{\sim\$50\text{B today}}$$

(= ~262M post-split shares × $192.50 = ~$50.4B; vs an ~$11B cost basis → ~$39B unrealized gain). **SpaceX upside flows through ~1:1**: ~+$58/SATS-share (÷347M) per +$1T of SpaceX value.

**Corrected SOTP NAV (mark-to-market, ÷347M diluted)**: SpaceX 2% ~$50B + AT&T net $20.25B + cash $1.34B + retained spectrum (AWS-3-heavy, encumbered) $8–22B + operating biz $0–6B − total debt $29.3B − taxes on the gain $10–12B − burn $2B → **bear ~$105 / base ~$133 / bull ~$168** (≈$126/$161/$202 on 288M basic). **vs $118 → a discount, not a premium.** My Stage-2 "fair-to-rich" call was wrong; Le Shrub's direction (deep discount to a SpaceX-linked NAV) was right.

**Why it still trades at a discount (the parts of my analysis that held)**: the stake is **locked, delivered ~Nov 30 2027** (17-mo SpaceX-valuation + survival risk); **going concern + skipped coupon** (the ~July 1 grace is a hard near-term default tail); large **tax drag**; the AT&T close is the de-risking event. So it is **both** a SpaceX-linked NAV play **and** a deal-timing/credit bet — the discount is the risk premium.

## What Worked

1. Going to **primary filings** (8-K / 10-K / 10-Q) instead of trusting the bull's bridge — caught the going concern, the skipped coupon, the real deal map, and ultimately the corrected mechanism.
2. Catching what Le Shrub **omitted** (going concern, the $183M skip, tax, the Nov-2027 lock) — those caveats survived the correction and are the real risk.
3. Once pushed, **re-verifying from the agreement** and changing the conclusion rather than defending the prior call.

## What I Got Wrong (Analyst Side)

1. **Asserted the consideration mechanism without verifying it.** "$X of stock **valued at $212/share**" with a **fixed** reference price means a **fixed share count** (share-anchored), not a fixed dollar value. I pattern-matched "a dollar figure appears" → "dollar-anchored." This is the headline error → **pitfall 26**.
2. **Split-basis hygiene failure.** Compared a pre-split reference ($212) to a post-split price ($192.50). A split is value-neutral; mixing bases produces a 5x error. The robust fix is to anchor on **ownership % × market cap** (split-invariant).
3. **Over-corrected against the bull.** Declared a real ~$50B stake a "phantom." Le Shrub's share count was right; only his price basis was off. Don't swing from "bull is right" to "bull is fabricating" — quantify.
4. **Ignored the market cross-check.** SATS tracking SpaceX's pre-IPO price increases was a clean revealed-preference signal of share-anchoring. I didn't look for it; the user had to supply it.
5. **Asserted an unsourced price.** Quoted SpaceX "$192.44" derived from "+20%," not pulled live. Pull the number (it later matched, but that's luck, not process).

## Reusable Framework: Valuing a Stock-Consideration Deal or a Pre-/Newly-Listed-Equity Stake

1. **Find the consideration definition in the primary agreement** (merger-agreement summary in the 8-K, or the deal note in the 10-K/10-Q). Do not infer it from a secondary write-up.
2. **Classify the anchor:**
   - **Share-anchored** (fixed share count) ⟺ a **fixed reference price** ("$X of stock **valued at $P/share**", P fixed at signing) → the recipient **bears the target's price risk/reward**; the target's moves **flow through 1:1**.
   - **Dollar-anchored / fixed-value** ⟺ price = a **closing-date VWAP / average** ("shares **with a value of $X** determined as of closing") → recipient gets **$X regardless**; target's moves **do not** flow through.
   - Collars/caps modify but don't erase this; read for them.
3. **Normalize the split basis.** A split is value-neutral. Never multiply a post-split share count by a pre-split price (or compare a pre-split reference to a post-split quote). When unsure, value via **ownership % × current market cap** — it's split-invariant.
4. **Cross-check against the tape.** If the proxy moves with the target's valuation, it's share-anchored; if it's indifferent, dollar-anchored. The market often reveals the mechanism before you finish reading the contract.
5. **Pull live prices** for both names; never assert a quote derived from a third party's "+X%".
6. **Then layer the discounts** that explain price vs NAV: delivery timing / lock-up, illiquidity, going-concern / deal-close hazard (pitfall 23), and **taxes on a low-basis gain**. The discount is the thesis's risk premium, not proof the asset is absent.
