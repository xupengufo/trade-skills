# VIX Call-Spread Market Hedge Case Study (June 2026)

A VIX call debit spread was recommended as a convex crash hedge while SPX sat near all-time highs with spot VIX at 15.9 and a steep-contango term structure. Same day, the market sold off (SPX −2.64%) and spot VIX spiked +39.7% to 21.51. This case tracks how two representative spreads *behaved* into that move — a nearer-dated **Jul 20/32** and a further-dated **Aug 20/30** VIX call debit spread (sizes/fills/P&L intentionally omitted — this is a structure-behavior study, not a position log). **Headline lesson: spot VIX +40% marked only ~+3–8% on these spreads** — a textbook demonstration that VIX options track the *future* (beta < 1), that the further-dated spread moves *less*, and that a debit spread's short leg suffers a *skew bite* on a vol spike. The structures behave correctly (green on a red day, convexity still ahead in the tail) — they are just not the moonshot a spot-VIX reading would imply.

---

## Setup

- **Instrument**: VIX options (cash-settled, European, **settle against the VIX future of their expiry — not spot VIX**)
- **Window**: 2026-06-05 (intraday)
- **Event**: broad-market risk-off day — SPX 7584 → 7384 (−2.64%); spot VIX 15.40 → 21.51 (+39.7%, +6.11 pts)
- **Starting context (at recommendation, ~spot VIX 15.9)**:
  - SPX 7584, YTD +10.8%, pinned near 52-week high (7620) — "high + calm"
  - Spot VIX 15.93, near 52-week low end (range 13.4–35.3)
  - **Steep contango term structure**: VIX9D ~12.3 → VIX(30D) 15.9 → VIX3M 19.2 → VIX6M 21.9
  - Implication: absolute VIX cheap (room to 35), but carry expensive (steep contango), and any back-month future already pricing ~18–22

## Strategy Evolution

### Stage 1: Recommendation (2026-06-05, spot VIX ~15.9)

**Thesis**: convex crash/tail hedge, not an efficient directional short. Framed explicitly: for a pure directional bearish view use SPY/SPX put spreads; VIX calls are for the *tail convexity* (market craters → vol explodes → spread multiplies).
**Recommended structure**: VIX **Aug 20/30** call debit spread (~75 DTE), est. net ~$1.85 per spread, max $10, ~5.4x. Variants offered: Jul 19/26 (cheaper/shorter), naked Aug 22 call (uncapped convexity), wide ladder.
**Mechanics warnings issued up front**: (1) anchor strikes to the future (~18.5 for Aug), not spot; (2) contango bleed; (3) futures beta < 1 — "model P/L off the future."

### Stage 2: Behavior into the spike (2026-06-05, spot VIX → 21.51)

As the market sold off, the structures were established / marked into a rising VIX. Per-contract moves on the day (illustrative market levels, not fills):

- **Jul 20C** (near the July future): ~2.41 → 2.77 = **+14.9%**
- **Jul 32C** (far-OTM right tail): ~0.78 → 1.01 = **+29.5%**

The far-OTM call's IV/price rose ~2× the near-the-money call's — the empirical **right-tail / skew steepening** that bites the short leg of a debit spread.

**Execution lesson**: adding into a *ripping* VIX pays escalating vol-of-vol on the later lots. Set full size at a calm entry, or wait for the curve to settle — do not chase the spike.

## Outcome (per-contract marks at 2026-06-05 close, spot VIX 21.51)

**Jul 20/32 spread**
- Entry ≈ long 20C 2.41 / short 32C 0.78 → **net debit ~1.63**
- Close ≈ long 20C 2.77 / short 32C 1.01 → net ~1.76
- **Marked ≈ +8% on the day**; max value at expiry (settle ≥ 32) = 12.00 pts; breakeven ≈ 21.63

**Aug 20/30 spread**
- Entry ≈ long 20C 3.32 / short 30C 1.43 → **net debit ~1.89**
- Close ≈ long 20C 3.48 / short 30C 1.53 → net ~1.95
- **Marked ≈ +3% on the day**; max value at expiry (settle ≥ 30) = 10.00 pts; breakeven ≈ 21.89

**Net: spot VIX +40% → only ~+3–8% marked on the spreads.**

### Why so small? (the whole lesson)

1. **Futures beta < 1.** Spot VIX +6.1 pts, but the back-month futures moved far less — the July future a few points, the August future even less. P/L tracks the future, not spot.
2. **Further-dated = lower beta.** Aug (+3%) *underperformed* Jul (+8%) on the identical move — the longer-dated future is stickier on a spot spike.
3. **Debit-spread skew bite.** The long Jul-20C gained +0.36 but the short Jul-32C gained +0.226 — the short leg captured **63%** of the long's move (far-OTM call IV ballooned more on the spike), so the net spread widened only +0.13.
4. **−2.6% is noise to this structure.** It is built for SPX −8%+ crashes where the future re-rates toward the strikes and the curve inverts. On a normal bad day it should be only mildly green — which is exactly what happened.

## What Worked

1. **Direction + vehicle correct**: the spreads were green on a red day. As insurance, they did their job.
2. **Defined risk**: a debit spread caps max loss at the net debit — no naked vol exposure.
3. **Low-IV core entry**: the core was placed at low absolute VIX (~16) before the spike fully priced in.
4. **Mechanics were pre-flagged**: the beta<1 / contango / future-anchor warnings meant the small first-day move was *expected*, not a shock — no panic, no premature exit.

## What I Got Wrong (Analyst Side)

1. **Should have quantified the "first-day" disappointment up front.** The original rec gave a crash-scenario P/L table but did not say "on a −2–3% day you'll be up low single-to-high single digits even if VIX +40%." That gap is exactly what a trader needs to not over-react.
2. **Should have warned against chasing the spike.** Adding into a ripping VIX = paying rising vol-of-vol. A "set full size at a calm entry or wait for the curve to settle" rule belongs in the original recommendation.
3. **Strike width / skew bite under-weighted.** A 20/32 (or 20/30) short leg is far enough OTM that its IV explodes on a spike. A *narrower* spread, an outright call, or a **2:1 long ratio** (buy 2 / sell 1 — stay net-long the right tail) would capture more of the move at higher carry. The trade-off was not made explicit. ## Reusable Framework: VIX Call-Spread Crash Hedge

1. **Decide the job.** Pure directional short → SPY/SPX put spread (linear, no contango). Convex crash/tail hedge → VIX call spread. Don't confuse them.
2. **Pull the term structure.** VIX9D / VIX / VIX3M / VIX6M. Steep contango → shorter-dated, spreads (not naked), smaller size.
3. **Anchor strikes to the future, not spot.** "20 call" ≈ ATM when the future is ~18.
4. **Pick DTE in the 45–75 day sweet spot.** Enough time to catch a move; carry not yet brutal. Nearer-dated has higher beta to a spike (Jul > Aug here).
5. **Size as insurance.** Expect to lose the premium in calm tapes; that is the cost of the option.
6. **Set full size at a calm entry — do not chase the spike.** Adding into a ripping VIX pays escalating vol-of-vol on the later lots.
7. **Build the P/L table off the future** with beta ~0.5–0.65 (lower further out). The crash column (SPX −8/−15%) is where the multiples live; the −2/−3% column should be only mildly positive — that's normal.
8. **Width / ratio is a skew decision.** Wide short leg cuts carry but suffers the most skew bite on a spike; narrow, outright, or a **2:1 long ratio** captures more first-day move and stays net-long the right tail at higher cost.
9. **Take profit fast.** In backwardation, book 60–70% of max value; don't wait for the cap — VIX reverts in days.
10. **Manage rolls sparingly** (pitfall 18). Each roll re-pays carry; only roll if the macro thesis is intact and the curve still favors it.
