## Lessons

### The actual trade — execution-vs-analysis gap (the real loss)

1. **I traded against my own written anti-rule.** The file literally said "❌ FOMO buy any -5% intraday dip — multi-week distribution still in progress," then I bought long stock on Day 1 near the close. Having the right analysis documented is worthless if the order ticket ignores it. The failure mode is *conviction leaking past the plan* — the hot-IPO tape is engineered to pull you in exactly when the plan says wait.
2. **No defined risk on a max-mania entry.** Long common stock into a +68% IPO-day close has no floor; the plan's whole point was to wait for *options* so risk could be defined. Going long the underlying threw away the one structural protection the framework was built around.
3. **The cut was the only thing that worked — honor it.** Exiting ~$290 turned a potential -17% into ~-5% to -7%. Cutting a thesis-violating position fast is the recovery move (pitfall 13). I did not average down or "wait for the bounce."
4. **Right thesis ≠ right trade.** Distribution played out exactly as modeled (-33.5% peak-to-5/22). Being analytically right while losing money is the most dangerous outcome because it feels like validation — it isn't.

### What I got wrong before the data came in

1. **Roadshow-range anchoring**: Modeled $160 upper bound; reality was $185. When pricing upgrades >50% from original midpoint, push my upper case one notch further.
2. **Primary vs fully-diluted share count**: First-pass valuation used primary stock outstanding. For IPOs, **always start with fully-diluted including warrants/RSUs/preferred conversion** before computing P/S or market cap.
3. **Under-modeled Day-1 mania**: Bull case had Day-1 +80%; actual peak +108%. Hot AI + revised-up IPO + retail FOMO can push 2x in hours.
4. **Bear-call-spread reflex was premature**: Without options + with ARK active demand, single-direction credit shorts have asymmetric squeeze risk early.

### What I got right

1. **Customer-concentration risk** identified before retail headlines caught it (86% UAE = a future headline waiting)
2. **Gross-margin ceiling thesis** (41% GM caps multi-year multiple expansion vs NVDA's 75%)
3. **Lock-up early-release detection** via S-1/A (earlier of Q3 earnings vs 180 days — most retail still assumes 180 days flat)
4. ~~**Patience discipline**: did NOT enter on Day-1.~~ **CORRECTED 5/25 — this is false.** The analytical record stayed patient, but the *actual trade* went **long stock on Day 1 (~$300-311)**, exactly the FOMO entry the plan banned. The only discipline that held was the **exit**: cutting at ~$290 (a ~-5% to -7% loss) instead of riding to $253 (~-17%). Right thesis, wrong execution on entry; the cut is what salvaged it. See the new lesson below.

### Meta-insight

The first 48 hours of a hot AI IPO are **not where the edge is**. Edge is at:
1. **Pre-IPO analytical preparation** (S-1 customer/margin reads no one else does)
2. **Greenshoe exercise window** (~day 5-25)
3. **Lock-up release window** (day 170-180, or earlier-of trigger if applicable)

The middle stretch (week 2 through month 3) is **vega/theta harvest territory, not directional**. Iron condors > directional spreads in that regime.

## Reusable Framework: Hot AI IPO Analysis (T-5 to T+60)

### T-5 to T-1 (pre-IPO)

1. **Get the S-1/A**, extract: customer concentration top-3, gross margin GAAP, fully-diluted shares including warrants, lock-up trigger language, capital structure (dual-class?), greenshoe size
2. **Track roadshow range moves**: every +25% upgrade = +1 notch on your upper case
3. **Compute fully-diluted market cap at each scenario** — never use primary
4. **Sector-relative P/S vs comparable**: anchor 1 mature peer + 1 hot peer; flag if IPO P/S > 2x mature peer

### T+0 (IPO day)

1. **Don't trade. Observe.** Watch open gap, intraday high, close-to-high distance
2. Record: gap %, intraday peak %, close %, intraday fade % (peak-to-close)
3. Volatility halt? Yes = retail-driven; No = orderly institutional accumulation

### T+1 to T+5

1. Day-2 reaction: if -5 to -15% on no news = distribution
2. Day-3 to Day-5: ARK / Cathie Wood appearance in news = bullish floor + bearish distribution marker (both signals)
3. Watch greenshoe 8-K typically days 2-7

### T+5 to T+10 (options-listing window)

1. **Do NOT touch options week 1 of listing** — IV opens 200%+, term structure noise > signal
2. Wait for IV term structure to invert (back-month > front-month settles to normal)
3. First structure: iron condor if range-bound; long put spread if continued distribution

### T+30 to T+60

1. Watch for ARK monthly disclosure → 1-week retail follow-on bid → fade
2. First 10-Q reveals OpenAI revenue recognition pace (or analogue customer)
3. Sizing window narrowest; first earnings reaction is the catalyst

### T+150 to T+180 (lock-up release)

1. **Highest-conviction directional window for hot IPOs**
2. Start positioning 2-3 weeks before earliest lock-up release
3. Structure: long puts 30-45 DTE through release date, OR bear call spread same expiry
4. Size 2-3x usual position because the setup probability is statistically the highest in IPO life cycle
